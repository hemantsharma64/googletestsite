Understanding Click Tracking with Google Analytics & Google Tag Manager

Reading time: ~5 minutes · Published on December 2, 2025

In this short guide, you’ll learn what click tracking is, why it is important for measuring engagement, and how tools like Google Tag Manager (GTM) and Google Analytics help you record every important action.

1. What is click tracking?

A normal pageview only tells you that someone opened a page. With click tracking, you also learn what they actually did: which buttons they pressed, which links they followed, and which call-to-action caught their attention.

On this page, when you click the blue button above, the click is recorded as a structured blog_click event in dataLayer. GTM reads that event and can forward it to your Google Analytics property as a custom event.

2. Why does click tracking matter?

Click data helps you answer questions such as:

  • Which call-to-action do users prefer in my email or landing page?
  • Do visitors scroll and interact, or do they bounce immediately?
  • Which links drive people to sign-up pages or pricing pages?

For email campaigns, you might send subscribers to a page like this. Every time they click the main button, you see that interaction inside Google Analytics and can measure the real performance of your campaign.

3. How does this demo page work?

At a high level, three simple steps happen:

  • Google Tag Manager is loaded on the page using the container ID.
  • When you click a tracked element, a custom event is pushed into dataLayer.
  • A GTM trigger listens for that event and fires a Google Analytics tag to record the event.

The event we push from this page looks roughly like this:

{ event: "blog_click", click_name: "visit_inboxdoctor", click_location: "hero_cta", click_text: "Visit InboxDoctor Site →" }

4. How do I see these clicks in Google Analytics?

Inside Google Tag Manager you would typically:

  • Create a Custom Event trigger for blog_click.
  • Create a GA4 Event tag that uses this trigger.
  • Publish the container, then open Realtime in Google Analytics to watch events appear as you click.

Once this is configured, every email you send that links to this page can generate valuable click data whenever the visitor interacts with the content or main button.

5. Summary

This simple educational page exists for one purpose: to help you understand and test click tracking. If you arrived here by clicking a link in an email, your pageview is already visible in Analytics. When you press the blue button, your click will appear there too.

Feel free to refresh, click again, and explore your analytics reports to see how each interaction is captured.
